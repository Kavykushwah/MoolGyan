MoolGyan

Agriculture is the hidden but quintessential STEM discipline where Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics are intricately interwoven into every aspect of human experience, and therefore can be the cornerstone of interdisciplinary and contextual experiential learning. This is even more pertinent and pronounced when seen in the light of the critical current need for healthy living, sustainable development, and global well-being. STEM linkage to agriculture is largely ignored by many outside of agriculture, which starkly reflects at all levels of formal education. Moolgyan is a project that aims to provide an innovative experiential way to connect middle- and high school students to STEM disciplines and sustainable living. Furthermore, Moolgyan is a platform to help us achieve nutrition and health for students through building intimate relationships with foods. In tune with many school garden programs that are a popular intervention for improving food and health behavior of school children, Moolgyan is built on the understanding that food ​behavior is established early in life. In order to influence children to make better food choices for a healthier lifestyle, Moolgyan activities are designed to give them hands-on experience in growing their own fresh produce, in combination with raising awareness about the importance of healthy diets. The first iteration of Moolgyan is underway where we are testing hypotheses on the impact of integrated STEM activities in a school vegetable garden on engaged student learning and understanding of healthy sustainable life choices.
